4th Grade Official Guide

Class Presidents

1 Peter 5:2-5

Shepherd the flock of God that is among you, exercising oversight, not under compulsion, but willingly, as God would have you; not for shameful gain, but eagerly; not domineering over those in your charge, but being examples to the flock. And when the Chief Shepherd appears, you will receive the unfading crown of glory.

Your 3 Jobs

Term: 2 Weeks  |  1 Boy + 1 Girl elected
1
Lead Morning Meeting
Call the class to order on time at 8:35am. Presidents will take turns leading the morning meeting activities. Talk about the word of the day, present student awards, call the pledge person to come up to do the pledge, and then go through the daily schedule.
2
Help Solve Problems
When classmates have a disagreement, they can choose to go to you to help them work it out calmly. Or Mrs. Williams might direct them to talk to you and get your help. You are a listener and a fair helper, not a boss.
3
Class Decision Voice
When the class gets to make a fun choice (like a game or activity), each president has the power to VETO one of Mrs. Williams's presented options. Then the co-presidents will deliberate briefly and decide whether to put it to a class vote or to choose on behalf of the class.

Remember: Presidents are leaders, not bosses. You cannot tell other students what to do. You serve the class, the class does not serve you.

Fair Election Process

1
During Monday Memo, everyone will write a "Campaign Promise." Anyone who wants to run raises their hand, and will have a chance to present their Campaign Promise to the class. TAs cannot run for President while they are a TA.
2
Each candidate presents their Campaign Promise to the class.
3
Each student will go to the ballot box and submit their vote secretly in the polling booth. Voting is private, no peaking.
4
You vote for ONE boy and ONE girl. You cannot vote for yourself. You cannot vote the same person twice.
5
Hit "Submit" on the screen, and confirm you vote. Then go sit down at your desk.
6
Ms. Williams counts the votes. The boy and girl with the most votes win. The winners will begin their term upon swearing in during the inauguration (Snack Time). Tie? Flip a coin.

Impeachment Process

Being President is a privilege. If you are not doing your job or you are breaking class rules, here is what happens:

Step 1: Warning Teacher gives you a private reminder. This is your chance to fix it. No one else needs to know.
Step 2: Final Warning If it happens again, the teacher tells the class that the President has received a final warning. You have one more chance.
Step 3: Removed If behavior continues, you are removed from the position immediately. The runner-up from the election takes over for the rest of the term. No vote needed.

Oath of Office

Mrs. Williams says: "Do you promise to be an example worth following -- in the way you speak, the way you listen, and the way you treat every person in this room?"

President-elect says: "I will."
